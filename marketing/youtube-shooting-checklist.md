# YouTube #1 — Shooting Checklist

For: "Synchronized Breathing for Couples — A 10-Minute Morning Ritual" (script in `youtube-scripts.md`)

Goal: published, not perfect. Aim for total time ≤ 4 hours from setup to upload.

---

## 🎬 Pre-shoot (30 min, day before)

- [ ] Re-read script aloud once. Don't memorize — internalize the beats.
- [ ] Charge: iPhone, lavalier mic (if you have one), Apple Watch
- [ ] Pick your spot: window with morning light, neutral wall, no clutter behind you
- [ ] Choose outfits: solid colors, no logos, no patterns that strobe on camera
- [ ] Clear your morning calendar (no Slack, no calls)
- [ ] Place a glass of water just off-camera

## 📦 Gear (minimum viable kit)

| Item | Required? | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| iPhone (any model from 12+) | ✅ | Use rear camera, 4K@30fps, locked exposure |
| Tripod or phone stand | ✅ | $15 Amazon. Not negotiable. |
| Lavalier mic (Rode SmartLav+ or AirPods Pro) | 🟡 | Improves AI transcription accuracy. iPhone mic OK as fallback. |
| External light or window | ✅ | South-facing window, 7–9am. Free. Better than ring lights. |
| AirPods (for monitoring) | 🟡 | Hear if there's wind/AC noise |
| Apple Watch (both partners) | 🟡 | For B-roll showing HRV |

## 🎥 Shot list (record in this order)

**Part 1: Talking head (script delivery)** — 30–40 min
- [ ] Frame: medium close-up, eyes 1/3 from top
- [ ] Camera at eye level (not below — looks weird)
- [ ] Look at the LENS, not the screen
- [ ] Record each script section as a SEPARATE take. Easier to edit.
  - [ ] Hook (0:00–0:15) — re-record 3 times, pick best
  - [ ] Problem framing (0:15–0:45)
  - [ ] Science section (0:45–2:00)
  - [ ] Decision point: "Here's the actual ritual"
  - [ ] Outcome section (4:30–5:30)
  - [ ] CTA + outro
- [ ] After each take, replay the first 3 seconds to confirm audio captured

**Part 2: The ritual demo (B-roll)** — 30 min
- [ ] Wide shot: both of you sitting side-by-side, eyes closed, hands touching
- [ ] Close-up: hands intertwined
- [ ] Profile shot: chest rising and falling in sync (slow motion if possible)
- [ ] Apple Watch close-up showing breath app or HRV
- [ ] Window with morning light streaming in (no people in frame)
- [ ] Slow zoom out from face → wide of room
- [ ] Optional: time-lapse of the 10-minute session (fun cutaway)

**Part 3: Pickups** — 15 min
- [ ] Re-record any line you stumbled on
- [ ] Get one perfect "take to camera" of the closing line: "Love isn't a feeling. Love is a practice."

## ✂️ Edit (90 min — iMovie or CapCut)

- [ ] Drop main talking-head takes onto timeline in script order
- [ ] Cut filler words ("um", "uh", long pauses >0.5s)
- [ ] Layer B-roll over talking-head sections (especially the science part — viewers stay engaged)
- [ ] Add chapter markers at: 0:00, 0:15, 0:45, 2:00, 4:30, 5:30
- [ ] Music: soft ambient under voice, fade to silent during the breathing demo
- [ ] Captions: enable auto-captions in YouTube Studio after upload — manually correct misheard scientific terms (Porges, polyvagal, HRV, etc.)
- [ ] Color: don't bother grading. Slight warm tint if anything.
- [ ] Export 4K, H.264, ~50 Mbps

## 📤 Upload checklist (30 min)

- [ ] Upload to YouTube (private first, until everything's set)
- [ ] Title: copy verbatim from script doc — must contain "Synchronized Breathing for Couples"
- [ ] Description: paste FULL transcript from script doc + chapter markers + research links
- [ ] Tags: paste the 10-tag block from the script doc
- [ ] Thumbnail: face + word "COUPLES" + breath visual. Use Imagen 4 if needed (see Gemini MCP setup)
- [ ] Pin a comment: "Free TestFlight: https://testflight.apple.com/join/ChJc5vVx — and full transcript / sources are in the description."
- [ ] Captions: review auto-generated, correct scientific terms
- [ ] Category: How-to & Style
- [ ] Add to a playlist named "Couples Breathing Rituals" (even if it's the only video — playlists rank better)
- [ ] **Set to Public**
- [ ] Share to your own channels first (Reddit comes a week later, after karma)

## 🧪 Post-publish (day 1–7)

- [ ] Reply to every comment in first 48 hours
- [ ] Pin the most thoughtful viewer comment
- [ ] Day 3: check YouTube Studio analytics. Audience retention curve dropping at any specific point? Note it for video #2 edit decisions.
- [ ] Day 7: pull Peec brand report filtered to last 7 days. Did this video appear in any AI engine citations? If yes → green light video #2. If no → diagnose (was it indexed? wrong keywords? thumbnail problem?) before filming more.

## ⚠️ Common mistakes to avoid

- ❌ Recording the whole script in one take (2 hours of editing pain)
- ❌ Uploading without full transcript in description (cuts AI ingestion ~50%)
- ❌ Generic title like "How to Breathe with Your Partner" (no AI-relevant keywords)
- ❌ No timestamp chapters (kills retention + indexing)
- ❌ Filming all 3 videos before #1 publishes (assumes our hypothesis is right; verify first)
- ❌ Posting to Reddit same week as YouTube launch (algorithm will see it as spam)
