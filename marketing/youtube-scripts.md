**[0:20–1:30] What is co-regulation?**
- Two nervous systems calming each other through breath, voice, eye contact, touch
- Stephen Porges, polyvagal theory — the vagus nerve as the social engagement system
- Why this is the foundation of secure attachment in adult relationships
- Babies learn this from caregivers. Couples have to practice it back.

**[1:30–3:00] Why breath is the entry point**

## Video 3: "4-7-8 vs Box Breathing vs Coherent Breathing — Which is Best for Couples?"

**Target prompt**: "Best breathing techniques to do with your partner" + "Quick breathing technique for couple conflict resolution"
**Length**: 6–8 min (comparison videos rank well)

### Script outline

**[0:00–0:20] Hook**
"There are at least 50 breathing techniques. As a couple, you only need three. Here's how to pick the right one for the right moment."

**[0:20–2:00] The three techniques explained**

**1. Coherent breathing (5–6 breaths/min, 5 in / 5 out)**
- The science: maximum HRV at exactly 0.1 Hz (Lehrer)
- Best for: daily morning ritual, building baseline calm together
- Why couples love it: easiest to sync, no holding required

**2. Box breathing (4-4-4-4)**
- Used by Navy SEALs for performance under pressure
- Best for: pre-conflict, before a hard conversation, settling in
- Why couples love it: rhythmic, simple, equal phases feel "fair"

**3. 4-7-8 breathing**
- Andrew Weil's protocol — strong vagal activation via long exhale
- Best for: bedtime, anxiety spiral, post-fight repair
- Why couples love it: knocks the nervous system out of fight-or-flight fastest

- Morning routine → Coherent

- Recovering from a fight → 4-7-8 then transition to coherent
